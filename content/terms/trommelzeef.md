# trommelzeef

## Dutch

**trommelzeef**  
Mechanisch scheidingsapparaat bestaande uit een **roterende trommel** (meestal om een horizontale as), voorzien van **perforaties of fijn gaas**, waarmee vaste deeltjes op basis van grootte worden afgescheiden uit een vloeistof- of slurry-stroom.

### Definitions

1. **Trommel**, om een horizontale as draaiend, bespannen met zeer fijn roestvrijstalen gaas; de afgescheiden vaste stoffen blijven aan de binnenzijde van de trommel achter en worden op het hoogste punt met waterstralen afgespoeld en opgevangen in een trog.  
2. **Draaiende zeef** met hoge rand voor continue mechanische afscheiding van vaste deeltjes.

**Reference:**  
ir. A. Koot, *Behandeling van Afvalwater*, Delft, pp. 271–272.

### Dutch variants and near-synonyms

- zeeftrommel  
- trommelzeef  
- roterende trommelzeef  
- draaiende zeef  
- draaizeef  
- trommel (context-dependent)  
- trommelzeel (variant spelling)

## English

### Preferred term (industrial and regulatory standard)

- **rotary drum screen**  
- **drum screen**

These are the **dominant and standard terms** in:
- wastewater treatment engineering,
- environmental technology,
- industrial screening equipment,
- EU and international technical documentation.

They are also the terms used in authoritative sources such as Wikipedia and IATE.

### Definition

**rotary drum screen**  
A **rotating cylindrical screen**, typically inclined slightly from the horizontal, used for mechanical size separation of solids from liquids or slurries.

Material is fed at the elevated end of the drum. As the drum rotates, solids are retained on the inner surface of the perforated shell while the liquid phase passes through the openings. Axial transport is governed by drum inclination and internal conveying elements, while radial movement is governed by rotational speed.

### Other English variants (attested, but less standard)

- rotary screen  
- revolving drum  
- revolving screen  
- cup screen  
- trommel screen / tromel screen (variant spellings)
- sieve drum  
- rotary sieve (rare; more common in laboratory or fine-sieving contexts)

⚠️ **Note:**  
Although *sieve* and *sieving* may appear in some technical or academic contexts (especially where perforation sizes are very small), **screen** is by far the most common and accepted term in industrial wastewater and environmental engineering English.

## Usage notes (important for translators)

In Dutch technical texts, *zeef* and *rooster* are often contrasted:

- **rooster** → coarse screen / bar screen  
- **zeef / trommelzeef** → fine screen / drum screen  

In English, both are typically rendered using **screen**, with the distinction expressed by modifiers such as:
- *coarse screen* / *bar screen*
- *fine screen*
- *drum screen*
- *micro-screen*

For this reason, translating *trommelzeef* as **rotary drum screen** best reflects **actual English engineering usage**, even though the Dutch root *zeef* might suggest *sieve*.
